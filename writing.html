Poem

snow, death and happiness

The snow falling blissfully,
The cold breath is getting colder each moment,
The white floor is starting to get red slowly,
There is a quiet and almost serene transition,
from unbearable pain to peace,
The tangled life is untangling itself,
The memory rushes to every corner of the brain,
accepting the new path,
The man lay down happily,
hoping he will be happy one day.

Fiction

Connor and Diana

24 September 1939


Dear Diana,


Many of my friends were killed today. They died fighting them. They all tried to stop the enemy, but it wasn't enough. I tried too, I swear to God I did, but they terrified me. Every time I looked at them, I thought of you, Diana.


I just couldn't leave you alone. I'm sorry, I was a coward. My family thinks I died alongside my friends; nobody knows that I ran away. I may still be breathing, but inside, I'm as lifeless as that chicken hanging in the butcher's shop. I'm too afraid to come back now. I can't face the families of the soldiers who died saving me while I ran away like a coward. I just can't, Diana.


The purpose of this letter is to confess one last time: I love you. I always have, and I always will. I would've surely married you, but I wasn't brave enough. Today, I'm going to meet my friends again. I don't know if they'll accept me up there, but I can't bear living with myself any longer.


I don't know if this message will ever reach you, but if it does, don't let it weigh on your heart.


Your lover,
Connor

Poem

She was magical

her voice was like the pure, cold, icy water falling from a waterfall,
her mere presence was nurturing and positive,
She was like the feeling of wonder and happiness that a child has,
the one person you can trust, even with your eyes pulled out,
She was the one.
But my mom always says god calls the good person as soon as possible,
Although she is not here physically, the echoes of her laughter still keep me awake at night, and the flowery fragrance of her hair still sits here with me
And I don't want to leave them alone ever again.

they're gone now,
what's left?
the flowers that used to bloom,
are now dull filled with darkness,
the mirrors are blind,
the tears are heavier than the eyes,
they all moved on,
but all i wanted was a goodbye.

Poem

i've had several funerals

I've had several funerals
some ceremonial
some sad
some even celebratory
But most of them
were quiet, private, and stoic
There were no grand prayers and weeping
It was just someone refusing to recognize me anymore.
I died thousands of times,
from different ways, with diverse personalities and under various names.
And not a single person knows.

Poem

The Grief

The Adobe that was flooded with tears a moon ago,
is turning back to a chattered dwelling.
It's not like they don't remember the reason for the crying,
It's just that they moved on, maybe a little too quickly
Some chose work to replace the emptiness
Some chose the few interim relations
And some just moved on
But some are still staring at the void
still thinking about all the things they could've done
But it's of no use, they too will soon move on
As life just moves on.
